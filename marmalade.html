Marmalade

 

Orange Marmalade 

Ingredients:

  • 2¼ lb (1 kg) Oranges  
  • 2 thin-skinned lemons  
  • 3½ pints (2.1 litres) water 
  • 3 lb (1.35 kg) granulated sugar 

 

Method:

  1. Scrub the oranges and lemons then cut them in half, squeeze the juice out of them. Add the juice to the water, then place the pips and any bits of pith that cling to the squeezer onto a square of muslin (laid over a dish or cereal bowl). Now cut the peel into quarters with a sharp knife, and then cut each quarter into thin shreds. As you cut, add the shreds to the water, and any pips or spare pith you come across should go on the muslin. The pith contains a lot of pectin, so don't discard any and don't worry about any pith and skin that clings to the shreds – it all gets dissolved in the boiling. 

  2. Now tie up the pips and pith loosely in the muslin to form a little bag, and tie this onto the handle of the pan so that the bag is suspended in the water. Then bring the liquid up to simmering point and gently simmer, uncovered, for 2 hours or thereabouts until the peel is completely soft – test a piece by squashing it against the side of the pan. At this point pop 3 small flat plates into the freezing compartment of the fridge. 

  3. Next remove the bag of pips and leave it to cool on a saucer. Then warm the sugar, pour it into the pan and stir it now and then over a low heat, until all the crystals have dissolved (check this carefully, it's important). Now increase the heat to very high, and squeeze the bag of pips over the pan (you'll see the pectin ooze out). You can do this by placing the bag between two saucers or using your hands. Then stir or whisk it into the rest. 

  4. As soon as the mixture reaches a really fast boil, start timing. Then after 15 minutes remove the pan from the heat, spoon a little of the marmalade on one of the cold plates from the fridge and let it cool back in the fridge. When it has cooled you can tell if you have a 'set' by pushing the mixture with your little finger – if it has a really crinkly skin, it is set. If not, continue to boil the marmalade and give it the same test at about 10-minute intervals until it does set. (If there's a lot of scum, most of it can be dispersed by stirring in half a teaspoon of butter, and the rest can be spooned off.) Leave the marmalade to settle for 20 minutes. 

  5. Finally pour the marmalade, using a funnel or a ladle, into the jars that have been washed thoroughly in warm soapy water, rinsed and dried, then warmed in a medium oven(325 F) for 5-10 minutes. Cover with waxed discs and seal while still hot. Label the jars when quite cold. 

     

Lemon Marmalade 

Ingredients:

  • 20 lemons (or oranges or mixed citrus)
  • 10 Mexican limes
  • Large piece of fresh ginger
  • Sugar

Method:

  1. Wash the lemons and limes. Quarter 10 of the lemons and remove the white core and seeds. 

  2. Slice the quartered pieces VERY thinly by hand or with a food processor and put in a large bowl. Juice the remaining lemons and add the juice to the sliced fruit. Zest the limes and juice them. Add this to the fruit mix. 

  3. Measure the fruit mix put it in the cooking pot, then add an equal amount of granulated sugar. Add five large slices of fresh ginger to the mix. 

  4. Stir until the sugar and fruit are well mixed and put on the stove. Bring the mix slowly to a full boil stirring frequently.In the meantime, put the canning jars, lids and rings, and the ladle and funnel into the large pots and add enough water to cover the jars etc. Put on high heat and boil for 20 minutes to sterilize the jars. 

  5. Boil the fruit mix until the mix reads 215 degrees fahrenheit on the candy thermometer, then remove it from the stove. Remove the jars from the waterbath using the jar gripper or tongs and drain them upside down on a rack for a minute. 

  6. Ladle the fruit mix into the sterilized jars(325 F – 5 minutes) and leave 1/4 inch of space at the top. Wipe the rim with a clean paper towel, put on a lid and a ring, and tighten securely. Set jars aside to cool.
